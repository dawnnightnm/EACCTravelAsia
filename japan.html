About Japan

The Land of the Rising Sun

Japan is a country that defies easy description — a place where bullet trains run to the second past century-old wooden temples, where neon-lit karaoke bars sit beside moss-covered zen gardens, and where a single bowl of ramen can bring you close to tears with its perfection.

From the cherry blossoms of Kyoto to the electric chaos of Tokyo's Shibuya crossing, Japan rewards every kind of traveller: the history lover, the foodie, the nature seeker, and the pop-culture enthusiast alike.

01 — Must-Visit Destinations

Iconic Places in Japan

Ancient temples, sacred mountains, bamboo groves, and glittering modern cities — Japan's beauty is boundless and breathtaking

Destination 01

Mount Fuji & Fuji Five Lakes

Yamanashi & Shizuoka Prefecture

Japan's sacred 3,776m volcano is the country's most iconic symbol. View it from Lake Kawaguchiko in spring for cherry blossom reflections, or attempt the summit climb July–September for a once-in-a-lifetime sunrise above the clouds.

Destination 02

Kyoto — City of a Thousand Temples

Kyoto Prefecture

Japan's ancient imperial capital houses over 1,600 Buddhist temples and 400 Shinto shrines. Visit Fushimi Inari's 10,000 vermillion torii gates at dawn, the golden Kinkaku-ji temple, and stroll the geisha district of Gion.

Destination 03

Tokyo — The World's Greatest City

Tokyo Metropolis

Stand at the famous Shibuya Scramble Crossing, explore Akihabara's anime culture, ride to the top of Tokyo Skytree, and eat your way through Shinjuku's memory lane. Tokyo has more Michelin-starred restaurants than any city on Earth.

Destination 04

Arashiyama Bamboo Grove

Western Kyoto

Walk through towering stalks of bamboo that filter sunlight into jade-green rays and create an otherworldly whisper in the breeze. Visit at sunrise before the crowds arrive. Nearby Tenryu-ji garden and monkey park make a perfect half-day trip.

Destination 05

Hiroshima & Miyajima Island

Hiroshima Prefecture

Visit the Peace Memorial Park and Museum for a profound, moving historical experience, then take a short ferry to Miyajima Island to see the "floating" Itsukushima torii gate rising from the sea at high tide — one of Japan's Three Views.

Destination 06

Nara — Deer City & Ancient Temples

Nara Prefecture

Over 1,200 semi-wild deer roam freely through this ancient capital alongside Japan's largest bronze Buddha (Daibutsu) housed in the massive Todai-ji temple. Feed the bowing deer with shika senbei crackers — they'll literally bow for a treat!

02 — Food & Cuisine

Must-Try Japanese Food

Japanese cuisine — or washoku — is a UNESCO-recognised culinary art. Precision, seasonal ingredients, and respect for every bite define this extraordinary food culture

Sushi & Sashimi

Vinegared rice topped with the freshest fish and seafood. For the purest experience, sit at the counter of a traditional Edomae sushi-ya and let the itamae chef guide your meal.

Ramen

Each region has its style: milky tonkotsu in Fukuoka, rich miso in Sapporo, soy-based shoyu in Tokyo. A steaming bowl of perfectly crafted ramen on a cold day is one of life's great joys.

Tempura

Gossamer-light battered and flash-fried seafood and vegetables. The secret is ice-cold batter and incredibly hot oil. Dip in tsuyu broth with grated daikon and enjoy the delicate crunch.

Takoyaki

Osaka's beloved street snack — crispy-outside, molten-inside balls of batter with octopus chunks, drizzled with mayonnaise, okonomiyaki sauce, and dancing bonito flakes. Impossibly hot. Irresistibly good.

Matcha Desserts

Japan's centuries-old tea ceremony culture expressed as soft-serve ice cream, tiramisu, mochi, Kit Kat, and dorayaki. Kyoto's Nishiki Market is the perfect place to work through every matcha variation imaginable.

03 — Travel Tips

Before You Go to Japan

Japan rewards well-prepared travellers. These tips will help you navigate the country with confidence and cultural grace

Best Time to Visit

Spring (March–May) for sakura cherry blossoms is magical but crowded. Autumn (October–November) offers vivid koyo fall foliage. Avoid Golden Week (late April–early May) if possible — it's extremely busy.

JR Pass

Buy a Japan Rail Pass before you arrive — it grants unlimited Shinkansen bullet train travel and is far cheaper than individual tickets. A 14-day pass suits most itineraries covering Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, and Hiroshima.

IC Card (Suica/Pasmo)

Get a Suica or Pasmo IC card at any major station. Use it for metro, buses, convenience stores, and vending machines nationwide. Japan remains largely cash-based — always keep ¥10,000+ handy.

Etiquette Essentials

Remove shoes before entering homes and some restaurants. Never tip — it can be seen as rude. Be quiet on public transport. Bow slightly when greeting. Eat, but don't walk while eating. Rubbish bins are scarce — carry a small bag.

Pocket WiFi & SIM

Rent a pocket WiFi router at the airport or buy a prepaid data SIM. Japan has excellent 5G coverage. Google Maps works perfectly and is the most reliable navigation tool inside Japan.

Wear Comfortable Shoes

You will walk 20,000+ steps per day. Wear shoes you can slip on and off easily for frequent temple visits. Many traditional restaurants and ryokan inns require removing shoes at the entrance.

The Spirit of Japan

Japan's culture is shaped by wa (harmony), omotenashi (selfless hospitality), and monono aware (the bittersweet beauty of impermanence). These values permeate every experience — from a perfectly prepared bowl of tea to the quiet reverence of a mountain shrine at dawn.
