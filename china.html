About China

The Middle Kingdom Awaits

China is one of the world's oldest continuous civilisations — a land where ancient imperial palaces stand beside futuristic skylines, where misty mountain peaks inspired a thousand paintings, and where street vendors serve dishes unchanged for centuries.

From the thundering majesty of the Great Wall stretching across northern mountains, to the serene karst landscapes of Guilin, China offers a travel experience unlike anywhere else on Earth. Each province is practically a country of its own, with distinct dialects, cuisines, and traditions.

"不到长城非好汉 — He who has not climbed the Great Wall is not a true hero."

01 — Must-Visit Destinations

Iconic Places in China

From the imperial grandeur of Beijing to the surreal landscapes of Zhangjiajie — China's wonders will leave you breathless

Destination 01

The Great Wall of China

Badaling & Mutianyu, Beijing

Stretching over 21,000 km across mountains and valleys, the Great Wall is humanity's most monumental construction. The Badaling section is most accessible; Mutianyu offers fewer crowds and stunning views. Visit at sunrise for an unforgettable experience.

Destination 02

The Forbidden City

Central Beijing

The world's largest palace complex, home to 24 emperors of the Ming and Qing dynasties. Stroll through 980 buildings, red-lacquered halls, and imperial gardens that were forbidden to ordinary citizens for five centuries.

Destination 03

Shanghai Bund & Skyline

Shanghai

The perfect collision of colonial-era architecture along the Bund and the glittering Pudong skyline across the river. Explore the French Concession, Yu Garden, and the world's fastest commercial maglev train.

Destination 04

Li River & Guilin

Guangxi Province

Cruise along emerald waters past towering karst limestone peaks that inspired classical Chinese ink paintings. The 83 km stretch from Guilin to Yangshuo is widely considered the most scenic river journey in all of China.

Destination 05

Zhangjiajie — Avatar Mountains

Hunan Province

The otherworldly sandstone pillars that inspired the floating Hallelujah Mountains in Avatar. Take the world's longest outdoor elevator (Bailong Elevator) for panoramic views, or cross the jaw-dropping glass-bottomed bridge above the gorge.

Destination 06

Terracotta Army — Xi'an

Xi'an, Shaanxi Province

Discover over 8,000 life-sized terracotta soldiers, horses, and chariots guarding the tomb of Emperor Qin Shi Huang since 210 BC. One of archaeology's greatest discoveries, and a UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1987.

02 — Food & Cuisine

Must-Try Chinese Food

Chinese cuisine is one of the world's great culinary traditions — bold flavours, ancient techniques, and infinite regional variety

Peking Duck

Beijing's most iconic dish — mahogany-roasted duck, crispy skin wrapped in pancakes with hoisin sauce and spring onion. A must-eat in the capital.

Dim Sum (Yum Cha)

The beloved Cantonese brunch tradition of small steamed dumplings, buns, and pastries served with tea. A social ritual as much as a meal.

Sichuan Hot Pot

A bubbling communal pot of numbing Sichuan peppercorn broth. Dip thinly sliced meats, vegetables, and tofu and revel in the bold heat of Chongqing's iconic dish.

Xiaolongbao

Shanghai's famous soup dumplings — delicate pleated wrappers hiding minced pork and a burst of hot, flavourful broth. Dip in vinegar with ginger and eat carefully!

Kung Pao Chicken

A beloved Sichuan stir-fry of tender chicken, crunchy peanuts, dried chilies and Sichuan peppercorns in a glossy, savoury-sweet sauce. Perfectly balanced heat and crunch.

03 — Travel Tips

Before You Go to China

Essential knowledge to make your China journey smooth, safe, and unforgettable

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–May) and Autumn (September–October) offer the most comfortable temperatures and clear skies. Summer is hot and crowded; winter is cold but crowd-free.

Visa Requirements

Most visitors require a Tourist Visa (L Visa) obtained from the Chinese embassy. Apply at least 2–3 weeks in advance. A 144-hour transit visa waiver is available at select ports.

Money & Payments

Currency is the Chinese Yuan (RMB/¥). Carry cash — many local stalls don't accept foreign cards. WeChat Pay and Alipay now accept foreign cards via tourist mode.

The Great Firewall

Google, Instagram, WhatsApp and many Western apps are blocked. Download a reliable VPN before you arrive. Apps like WeChat and Baidu Maps work perfectly inside China.

High-Speed Rail

China has the world's largest high-speed rail network. Book tickets in advance on the 12306 app. A Beijing–Shanghai trip takes only 4.5 hours on the Fuxing bullet train.

Language Tips

Mandarin is the official language. English is limited outside major hotels and tourist sites. Download Google Translate or Pleco offline for daily communication and menu reading.

Embrace Chinese Culture

China's culture is a rich tapestry of Confucian values, Taoist philosophy, and Buddhist traditions. When visiting temples, dress modestly. Offer and receive gifts with both hands. Slurping noodles is perfectly polite — it shows appreciation for the chef!
