About Korea

The Land of the Morning Calm

South Korea is a country of astonishing contrasts — ancient palaces rise in the shadow of glass skyscrapers, Buddhist monks walk past K-pop music shops, and traditional markets selling fermented kimchi and crispy pancakes operate next to Michelin-starred restaurants.

Known globally today for K-pop, K-dramas, and K-beauty, Korea has a cultural depth that stretches back thousands of years. Its cuisine is some of Asia's most complex and delicious, its people some of the most warm and hospitable, and its landscapes — from volcanic Jeju Island to the neon-lit streets of Seoul — spectacularly diverse.

01 — Must-Visit Destinations

Iconic Places in Korea

Royal palaces, island paradises, colourful hillside villages, and a capital city that never sleeps — Korea has something extraordinary at every turn

Destination 01

Gyeongbokgung Palace

Jongno-gu, Seoul

The grandest of Seoul's Five Grand Palaces, built in 1395 as the main palace of the Joseon dynasty. Watch the ceremonial Changing of the Royal Guard, then rent a hanbok (traditional dress) to get in free and roam the majestic courtyards.

Destination 02

N Seoul Tower & Namsan

Yongsan-gu, Seoul

Perched on Namsan Mountain, N Seoul Tower offers 360° panoramic views of the sprawling capital. The hilltop is ringed with thousands of love padlocks. Take the cable car up, or hike the forested Namsan trail at sunset for stunning city views.

Destination 03

Bukchon Hanok Village

Jongno-gu, Seoul

Over 900 traditional Korean hanok houses with elegant curved rooflines line this hilly neighbourhood between Gyeongbokgung and Changdeokgung palaces. Explore the narrow alleys, discover craft workshops, and step back 600 years into Joseon-era Seoul.

Destination 04

Jeju Island

Jeju Special Self-Governing Province

Korea's volcanic island paradise — a UNESCO Triple Crown site. Hike Hallasan (Korea's highest peak), dive into crystal-clear Manjanggul lava tube caves, wonder at the Seongsan Ilchulbong sunrise peak, and swim at Hamdeok's turquoise beach.

Destination 05

Busan — Korea's Coastal Capital

Busan Metropolitan City

Korea's second city delivers beach life at Haeundae, the rainbow-painted Gamcheon Culture Village (the "Machu Picchu of Busan"), the freshest seafood at Jagalchi Market, and temple-cliffside solitude at Haedong Yonggungsa.

Destination 06

Seoraksan National Park

Gangwon Province

Korea's most celebrated national park, where jagged granite peaks, ancient Buddhist temples, and crystal streams create scenery that explodes into magnificent red and gold in autumn. The Ulsanbawi Rock sunrise hike is simply unmissable.

02 — Food & Cuisine

Must-Try Korean Food

Korean food is bold, fermented, spicy, and utterly addictive. Every meal comes with an array of banchan (side dishes) — making every table a feast

Korean BBQ (삼겹살)

Grill thick-cut pork belly (samgyeopsal) or marinated beef (galbi) over charcoal at your table. Wrap in perilla leaves with garlic, kimchi, and gochujang paste. The most social meal in Korea.

Bibimbap (비빔밥)

A colourful bowl of rice topped with seasoned vegetables, marinated beef, a fried egg, and a generous spoonful of gochujang red pepper paste. Mix everything together and enjoy one of Korea's most iconic dishes.

Tteokbokki (떡볶이)

Chewy cylindrical rice cakes simmered in a fiery, sweet gochujang sauce — the quintessential Korean street food. Found at every pojangmacha street stall. Pairs perfectly with eomuk fish cakes and a sip of Korean soda.

Japchae (잡채)

Silky sweet potato glass noodles stir-fried with rainbow vegetables, beef, and a savoury-sweet sesame soy glaze. Often served at celebrations, it's one of Korea's most beloved comfort foods and a staple at every family table.

Korean Fried Chicken

Double-fried for unparalleled crunch, then glazed in sweet-spicy gochujang or soy garlic sauce. Best enjoyed with a cold bottle of makgeolli rice wine in a Chimaek (chicken + maekju/beer) restaurant. Pure bliss.

03 — Travel Tips

Before You Go to Korea

Korea is one of Asia's most visitor-friendly destinations. These tips will help you get the most from your Korean adventure

Best Time to Visit

Spring (April–May) for cherry blossoms and vibrant festivals, and Autumn (September–November) for stunning fall foliage in national parks. Summer is hot and humid; winter is bitterly cold but magical with snow and fewer tourists.

T-Money Card

Pick up a T-money card at any convenience store or station. Top it up and use it on all metro, buses, and even some taxis across Korea. Also works in convenience stores like GS25 and 7-Eleven — an absolute must-have for every visitor.

Kakao Map & Naver

Google Maps has limited transit routing in Korea. Use Kakao Map or Naver Map instead — both have English interfaces and provide accurate subway and bus directions. Download offline maps before you go.

K-Culture Experiences

Visit HYBE Insight, SM Town Museum, or Starfield Library for K-pop immersion. See a BTS mural in Dongdaemun, explore Hongdae's indie music scene, or attend a Korean drama filming location tour in Jeonju's Hanok Village.

Jjimjilbang (찜질방)

Korea's legendary 24-hour heated bathhouse and sauna complexes are a must-experience. For under ₩15,000, enjoy traditional kiln saunas, cold plunge pools, and rest areas. A beloved local ritual for relaxation and recovery.

Shopping & Beauty

Myeongdong, Hongdae, and Dongdaemun are shopping paradises. Korea is the global capital of skincare — stock up on affordable sunscreen, sheet masks, and serums at Olive Young. K-beauty products make perfect souvenirs.

The Soul of Korea

Korean culture is rooted in Confucian values of filial piety, collective harmony, and deep respect for elders. The concept of nunchi (social awareness) shapes all interactions. Koreans eat together, play together, and celebrate together — hospitality is not a custom, it's a way of life.
